2. JUMPER METHODS REFERENCE — ladder model + all methods

   docs/v3-planning/JUMPER-METHODS-REFERENCE.md

   Authoritative per-method spec for jumpers. Methods covered:
     0 (Table III), 2 (II.2a), 3 (2-round + JO), 4 (II.1 Speed),
     5 (Top Score — dropped), 6 (IV.1 Optimum), 7 (Timed Eq),
     8 (dropped placeholder), 9 (II.2d), 10 (II.2f Stratified),
     11 (II.2c — THIS is the II.2c, not a modifier of method 9),
     13 (II.2b Immediate JO), 14 (Team), 15 (Winning Round).
   Methods 1 and 12 are complete unknowns (flagged).

   Bill's LADDER MODEL: a ladder between rounds exists ONLY
   when faults are cumulative. Ladder = later-round EL wipes
   earlier result. Methods with ladders: 3 (R1↔R2), 9 (PH1↔PH2),
   14 (R1↔R2). Everything else is ladder-free.

   Monotonic stacks — an entry's tier never decreases. Either
   holds tier or removed entirely.

10. ARCHITECTURAL PRINCIPLES — hardware type vs class_kind

    Big architectural clarification: the classType column
    (J/T/H/U) is really HARDWARE TYPE, not class kind. Any
    scoring method can run on Farmtek (J) or TOD (T) hardware.
    v3 descriptor MUST split:
      hardware_type: 'farmtek' | 'tod' | 'unknown'
      class_kind:    'jumper'  | 'hunter' | 'equitation'
    Derived independently. Parser should never refuse extraction
    because hardware_type is unknown.

    TIMY is dead hardware. T classType now means TOD.

    TOD = Time of Day = FEI's absolute-reference-clock timing
    model. Our v1.11 heartbeat-as-authority was Bill's original
    port of this philosophy to the web.
